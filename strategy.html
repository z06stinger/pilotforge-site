Rerouting Rights — Know Your Contract
A pilot rerouted on day 3 of a 5-day trip
Pilot
I'm on day 3 of a 5-day. Yesterday they rerouted me to DAL instead of CHA. This morning they left a CNO trying to reroute me again instead of returning me to my original rotation. What are my options?
Atlas
Your instinct is right. PWA Section 23 L. (Rerouting) governs this:

After a reroute, the Company must either:
1. Return you to your original rotation at the earliest opportunity, or
2. Release you if they cannot return you

They cannot chain reroutes. What you're describing — rerouted to DAL, now rerouted again — is exactly the scenario the PWA protects against.

Your options:
• Do NOT acknowledge the CNO yet
• Document everything — screenshots, timestamps
• Contact your union rep for real-time support

Per Section 4 F. (Rotation Guarantee), your pay is protected regardless — you're entitled to the greater of your original scheduled rotation or what you actually fly.
What happened: A stressed pilot in a hotel room needed a clear answer fast. Atlas cited the exact PWA sections, laid out specific actions in priority order, and flagged the pay protection angle most pilots wouldn't think of in the moment. No searching through a 500-page contract required.
New Hire — Understanding FDP Limits
A first-year pilot learning the rules
Pilot
I'm new — what's my FDP limit for a 3-leg day reporting at 0600?
Atlas
Per FAR §117.13, Table B, acclimated, 0500-0559 report window with 3 flight segments:

Your FDP limit: 12.0 hours

That means if you report at 0600, your FDP window closes at 1800.

Extension available: Per §117.19, the PIC may extend FDP by up to 2 additional hours (to 14.0h) with agreement from all flightcrew members, if the PIC determines the crew is fit.

Key things to know as a new hire:
• FDP starts at report time, not block out
• FDP includes taxi, flight, and post-flight duties
• The segment count matters — more legs = lower FDP limit
• If you're ever unsure, you have the right to decline an extension
